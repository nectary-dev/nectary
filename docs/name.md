# Why "Nectary"

A **nectary** is the organ of a flower that produces nectar — the structure that secretes the incentive.

The flower doesn't pay the bee a wage, and the bee doesn't own the flower. The flower produces nectar; the nectar attracts bees; the bees keep the flower reproducing; the cycle sustains itself. The nectary is the small, specific piece of anatomy that makes the whole loop run.

That is exactly the role this project plays for a repository:

| Metaphor | System |
|---|---|
| Flower | The repo / the idea being materialized |
| Nectar | Capped, revenue-repaid cost claims |
| Nectary | This protocol — the structure that secretes the incentive |
| Bees | Contributors: developers and AI agents |
| Pollination | Completed, verified deliverables |
| The cycle | A self-sustaining, self-funding repo |

## What the metaphor is allowed to mean

The metaphor describes **incentive-cycle infrastructure**: a repo that can attract and reward the effort needed to build itself. It deliberately points at the structure that enables bootstrapping and community participation — not at the cash.

It must **not** be stretched to imply ownership, profit-sharing, speculative upside, or a revenue share. A bee visiting a flower is compensated for the trip; it does not acquire equity in the garden. See [design-principles.md](design-principles.md), principle 1.

## Why not "Nectar"?

The obvious shorter name collides heavily with existing Web3 identities (Nektar Network, NEC, NECT, and others). Money-flavored names were rejected too: the public story is about the cycle, the community, and the bootstrapping mechanism — not the money. "Nectary" names the organ, which is precisely what this project is: not the reward itself, but the structure that produces it.

The project lives at [nectary.dev](https://nectary.dev).
